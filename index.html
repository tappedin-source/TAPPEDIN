The Story

Built by
one pair
of hands

Every jacket starts as fabric on a table and ends as something you'll wear for decades. There are no assembly lines, no overseas factories, no compromises on material.

I started sewing jackets because I couldn't find what I wanted to wear. Something that felt both refined and real. Something that showed the work behind it.

Each piece takes between 20 and 40 hours to complete. The stitching is done by hand. The patterns are made by hand. The result is something that couldn't exist any other way.

The Craft

How every
jacket is
made

No shortcuts, no machines doing the important work. Here's what goes into each piece.

01
Pattern & Cut

Every pattern is drawn and cut by hand. Shapes are refined over multiple iterations until the proportions feel right. Nothing is rushed.

02
Material Selection

Fabrics are chosen for longevity and feel. I source from mills that prioritize quality over output. Every jacket uses material worth wearing for a decade.

03
Hand Stitching

The stitching is done entirely by hand. This is the slowest part and the most important. The seams are what hold everything together — literally and aesthetically.

04
Finishing & Inspection

Every edge, button, and seam is inspected before a jacket leaves. If something isn't right, it doesn't go out. Simple as that.
